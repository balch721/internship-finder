Background

Who's behind this

hi — I'm Aryan, an engineering student at Purdue from Monroe, New Jersey, with a background in research and entrepreneurship. I like building things that remove guesswork — and internship season is nothing but guesswork: thousands of postings, dead links, hidden deadlines, and no sense of where you'd actually fit.

Telos is my answer — built nights and weekends, researched company by company.

The project

What Telos does

Take a quick survey for a ranked, personalized shortlist — or explore the whole database by field. Every listing is researched with real company details: tiers, competitiveness, estimated pay, company values, stock and recent news, plus the majors each role actually recruits — so your matches reflect where you'd genuinely fit.

Application links are checked against the real postings, deadlines are tracked, and the database refreshes three times a week. It's a work in progress, with more fields going live over time.

Why “Telos”

télos — Greek, “the end toward which a thing aims.”

Aristotle used télos for the purpose built into a thing — the destination it exists to reach. That's exactly what an internship search should feel like: not scrolling for whatever's posted, but aiming at the role you're actually for. The name is the mission — and the reason the homepage points you at one lit summit instead of ten thousand dark ones.
